Executive Summary

Humaniti delivered $43.7M in gross donations across 2025 at a 3.24× ROAS, generating $30.3M net. The platform entered 2026 with its strongest-ever recurring base — 44,258 active recurring donors at Ramadan 2026 peak — and an MRR run-rate of $1.58M ($18.9M ARR). Ramadan remains the dominant revenue event, but the platform has materially de-risked through recurring revenue growth: monthly MRR has climbed from $1.10M in January 2025 to $1.58M in March 2026, a 44% increase in 15 months.

Channel Insights

  • 1
    Meta is the primary acquisition engine ($15.37M, 35.1%) but its Ramadan efficiency dropped sharply YoY (-28.7%), suggesting rising CPMs or creative fatigue. Diversification is urgently needed.
  • 2
    Google is rock-solid ($12.69M, 29.0%) and nearly flat YoY during Ramadan (-1.0%). Google's demand-capture model is more resilient to donor fatigue than Meta's push model.
  • 3
    SMS is growing fast ($3.53M in 2025, +20.8% at Ramadan 2026). At $1.40M in a single Ramadan month, SMS is becoming a major retention and reactivation channel. Investment here is clearly compounding.
  • 4
    Email is under-indexed at only 6.9% ($3.0M). For a platform with 707K unique donors, email revenue of $3M implies extremely low email engagement rates or low send volumes. This is a significant missed opportunity.
  • 5
    "Other" at 17.8% ($7.78M) is unattributed revenue — likely including direct traffic, dark social, and untagged links. This needs a UTM audit. Any meaningful portion of this is recoverable to a known channel.

Section 7

Strategic Findings

  • 1
    The recurring flywheel is working. MRR grew +44% in 15 months without a step-change in recurring programme investment. Each Ramadan permanently raises the floor. The platform has crossed a threshold where recurring revenue ($16.6M in 2025, 37.9% of gross) is large enough to sustain baseline operations even in a low-acquisition month.
  • 2
    Ramadan is both the greatest opportunity and greatest risk concentration. March contributes 22.1% of annual gross in a single month. The 2026 Ramadan decline of 5.8% gross revenue — even while recurring MRR grew 19.8% — illustrates how one-time donation softness can mask structural improvement. Measurement frameworks should separate recurring from one-time at all times.
  • 3
    New donor acquisition is deteriorating at the margin. Cost per new donor rose from $55 at Ramadan 2025 to $83 at Ramadan 2026 (+49.5%), while new donor volume fell 40.6%. This is the most urgent strategic issue: without fresh new donor intake, the recurring base will naturally churn over time. The platform needs a structural new-to-file acquisition strategy beyond Ramadan and paid media.
  • 4
    Meta efficiency is declining; SMS is the standout growth channel. Meta Ramadan revenue fell 28.7% YoY despite similar or greater spend levels. SMS grew 20.8% at Ramadan 2026. The channel mix needs deliberate rebalancing toward SMS, email, and WhatsApp — direct channels with higher conversion quality and lower auction-based cost inflation.
  • 5
    $7.78M (17.8%) of 2025 revenue is unattributed. This "Other" bucket represents a significant blind spot in performance management. A structured UTM governance framework could reclassify the majority of this into known channels, enabling better budget allocation decisions.
  • 6
    Email is massively under-exploited. With 707K+ unique donors on file in 2025 alone, email revenue of $3.0M (6.9%) implies very low email list utilization or engagement rates. A systematic email programme — welcome series, recurring upgrade sequences, lapsed donor reactivation — could realistically add $2–4M in annual revenue at near-zero incremental acquisition cost.

Section 8

Strategic Recommendations

Six priority actions ranked by impact on sustainable long-term revenue growth.

01
Build a Year-Round New Donor Acquisition Engine
The 40.6% decline in new donors at Ramadan 2026 is a lagging indicator of insufficient acquisition investment outside of peak events. Implement a structured always-on acquisition programme targeting 8,000–12,000 new donors per non-Ramadan month (currently averaging ~14K/month in 2025 but falling to ~10K in H2). Prioritise channels with lower auction pressure: Google non-brand, YouTube pre-roll, and TikTok (currently near-zero but untested at scale).
02
Launch a Structured Email Revenue Programme
707K unique donors in 2025, $3.0M in email revenue — implying an average email revenue per donor of ~$4.24. Best-in-class nonprofit email programmes generate $15–25 per donor annually. A welcome series, monthly recurring upgrade sequence, lapsed donor reactivation (targeting the 465K returning donors), and campaign-specific appeals for non-Ramadan events could realistically add $3–6M annually. The infrastructure cost is marginal relative to the return.
03
Scale SMS as the Primary Retention Channel
SMS grew 20.8% at Ramadan 2026 and is the only major channel showing consistent positive momentum. At $1.40M in a single Ramadan month and $3.53M across 2025, SMS is proving its value — but it is still only 8.1% of revenue. Doubling SMS investment and building automated SMS journeys (recurring upgrade, lapsed reactivation, event-triggered giving prompts) should be a 2025 priority. Target: $6–8M SMS revenue in 2026 across all months.
04
Reduce Meta Dependency Through Channel Diversification
Meta went from $2.84M to $2.02M at Ramadan — a $813K drop — while spend levels remained significant. This is not a budget problem; it is a reach/frequency saturation problem. The audience has been over-exposed. Introduce creative rotation at a minimum 6-week cadence, expand lookalike pools, and redirect 15–20% of Meta budget to YouTube and TikTok to access untapped audiences. Target Meta share below 30% of revenue by end of 2026.
05
Implement UTM Governance to Recover the $7.78M "Other" Bucket
17.8% of 2025 revenue ($7.78M) has no clear channel attribution. Even recovering 50% of this into known channels would materially change budget allocation decisions. Audit all active campaign links for UTM completeness, implement a UTM naming standard across all teams, and set a target of <5% unattributed revenue by Q2 2026. This is a data hygiene fix, not a spend decision — and it is free.
06
Formalise a Recurring Donor Upgrade Programme
The platform has 38,000–44,000 recurring donors at any given time. An upgrade programme — targeting the bottom 30% of recurring donors (likely giving $5–15/month) with a personalised upgrade ask — could increase average recurring gift from ~$29 to $35+. A $6 average MRR increase across 30% of 40K donors = $72,000 additional MRR ($864K ARR) from zero new acquisition. This is the highest ROI initiative available at current scale.
