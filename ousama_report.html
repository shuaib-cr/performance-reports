How to Read This Report

Two Analytical Views, Clearly Labelled

This report draws on two complementary views of the same attribution data. Every section and chart is tagged so you always know which lens is in use.

📅 Monthly View
Point-in-time snapshot per calendar month
  • All MRR and ARR figures in this report come exclusively from this view. Each month's MRR reflects only the recurring donations actually processed in that calendar month.
  • Recurring donor counts = unique donors active in that specific month — not a cumulative total.
  • Best for: MRR trajectory, ARR run-rate projections, seasonal growth patterns.
  • Does not break down by campaign — all programmes are merged into one monthly row.
📊 Annual Campaign View
Aggregated Jan 2025 – Apr 2026
  • One aggregated row per campaign across the full period.
  • Enables programme ranking, Humaniti vs non-Humaniti classification, cross-sell detection, and English/Arabic split.
  • Recurring donor counts are cumulative — a donor giving to Water and Palestine both appears in both campaign rows, so the total exceeds the true unique headcount.
  • Best for: campaign ranking, programme mix, channel breakdown, platform attribution.

Section 9 — Query Audit

Attribution Query — Optimizations & Logic Notes

The underlying BigQuery queries that produce these reports are logically sound and produce reliable outputs. The following observations cover performance and analytical accuracy improvements worth prioritising.

Performance Optimizations
Attribution CASE block duplicated 3×. The influencer name resolution logic appears identically in content_match_count, campaign_match_count, and the final CASE. Extracting to a single CTE reduces SQL length ~40% and halves per-row evaluation cost.
LOWER() applied at query time on all text fields. Pre-normalising to lowercase at ingestion (or in a staging view) removes repeated per-row function calls across large tables.
Month spine CROSS JOIN fans out at scale. The monthly view generates influencer × month combinations before filtering — fine for one influencer but inefficient for multi-influencer runs. Filter influencer_list before the CROSS JOIN.
first_donation_date window scans the full raw table. MIN(dt) OVER (PARTITION BY email) runs on every query execution. Pre-computing in a materialised view would significantly reduce compute cost.
SAFE_CAST and SAFE_DIVIDE used throughout. No division-by-zero risk, no silent failures on malformed numeric fields.
MRR multiplier factors are correct and consistent across both views (daily ×30.4375, weekly ×4.33333, monthly ×1.0, quarterly ×0.33333, annual ×0.08333).
Logic & Analytical Accuracy
No cross-campaign deduplication in the annual view. A donor active in Water and Palestine appears in both rows. The 16,256 cumulative total should not be used as a unique headcount — use the monthly view's 7,694 for that purpose.
muslimi - palestine carries a Humaniti campaign code. This classifies it as Humaniti in code-based filters despite its name. Explicit override logic (or a documented precedence rule) should be defined and applied consistently.
new_donor definitions differ between the two views. Annual view: checks if first_donation_date falls in the full date range. Monthly view: checks if it falls in the same calendar month. These produce different counts for the same cohort — do not compare directly.
April 2026 data is partial. The monthly view includes April 2026 mid-month — MRR and ARR figures for that row are understated and should be excluded from run-rate projections. Use March 2026 as the current baseline.
Arabic detection is comprehensive — covers campaign name, donation page URL, utm_campaign, and utm_content with multiple pattern variants. Good defensive coverage against inconsistent tagging in source data.

Section 10

Strategic Recommendations

Five priority actions ranked by expected impact on sustainable MRR growth.

01
Protect Water — and Actively Reduce Its Dominance
Water is 36.2% of all revenue and 37.5% of all cumulative recurring records. This single-programme concentration is the greatest structural risk. MRR has reached a $2.33M ARR run-rate — protecting this requires diversifying the recurring base so that no single programme's disruption collapses the curve. Redirect 10–15% of Water budget to Palestine, Hot Meals, and Orphans without reducing Water's absolute spend.
02
Scale Orphans — Highest Pledge Quality
Orphans generates disproportionate recurring value per conversion — high average pledge size against a low volume of records (286 cumulative). A dedicated Orphans content investment at 10–15% of Water's budget could materially raise the overall MRR-per-active-donor above the current $25.22, improving the quality of the recurring base without needing to grow headcount proportionally.
03
Formalise the Masjid Fund / Al Abrar Cross-Sell
$70,937 and 228 recurring records have been generated through the "additional" campaign structure organically. Build a systematic post-donation cross-sell journey: Water and Palestine monthly donors receive a dedicated Masjid Fund / Al Abrar ask at the 30-day mark. A 5× scale-up from the current organic baseline is realistic within two Ramadan cycles given existing conversion evidence.
04
Invest in Arabic Content at Scale
Palestine Arabic converts at 26.3% recurring — 6.8 points above the English equivalent — yet Arabic content represents only 16.1% of total revenue. Dedicated Arabic content for Orphans, Sudan, and Refugees (mirroring the English programme structure) could unlock $400–600K in additional annual revenue with a higher recurring mix than equivalent English investment.
05
Build a Retention Channel to Protect the MRR Base
With MRR at $194K/month (ARR $2.33M) and 95.5% of revenue flowing through Meta and Google, the compounding recurring base now sits on a fragile paid-platform foundation. With 92,599 donors acquired, the audience justifies owned-channel investment: structured SMS/email sequences for attributed donors, a YouTube retargeting layer, and a WhatsApp opt-in programme. Target: shift 3–5% of revenue to owned channels within 12 months without reducing MRR growth rate.
