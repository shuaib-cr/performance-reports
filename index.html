Section 7

Strategic Recommendations

Five priority actions ranked by expected impact on MRR growth and donor lifetime value.

01
Invest in Arabic Content at Scale
Arabic content represents 22.7% of revenue with a higher recurring rate (21.8% vs. 19%) and a materially higher average gift ($51–73 vs. $42–52). The Arabic audience is being under-served — only 3 dedicated Arabic campaigns exist vs. 9+ English ones. A dedicated Arabic content calendar for Ousama, mirroring his top English programs (Orphans, Refugees, Sudan) in Arabic, could unlock $400–600K additional annual revenue at current conversion rates.
02
Build a Post-Acquisition Recurring Upgrade Journey
Ousama delivers 68K+ new donors annually — but 88.6% are first-time givers with no structured path to recurring. Implementing a 30/60/90-day email and SMS sequence specifically for Ousama-attributed new donors, with a recurring upsell offer, could realistically convert an additional 3–5% to monthly giving, adding $800K–$1.3M in ARR based on the current cohort size and average recurring gift.
03
Expand Masjid Fund / Al Abrar Cross-Sell Intentionally
$59,790 and 148 recurring donors have already flowed into Masjid Fund / Al Abrar through Ousama's content organically. A structured cross-sell programme — a dedicated "additional" ask embedded into Humaniti Water or Palestine donor journeys — could scale this 5–10x. The 24.4% recurring rate on existing Masjid Fund / Al Abrar donors sourced through Ousama confirms strong audience alignment.
04
Scale the Orphans Programme — Highest Recurring Conversion
Humaniti — Orphans has a 25.5% recurring rate — the highest of any English campaign — yet generated only $216K vs. $2.85M for Water. Ousama's content clearly resonates with orphan sponsorship messaging. A dedicated paid media investment in this programme at 10–20% of Water's budget could yield $200–400K additional revenue with outsized recurring characteristics.
05
Diversify Channel Mix to Reduce Meta Dependency
95.6% of revenue concentrates across just two paid platforms. Ousama's existing audience size (77K+ donors) justifies building a retention channel: a structured SMS programme (only $101K currently), a YouTube retargeting layer, and a weekly email digest could each contribute meaningful incremental revenue while reducing platform concentration risk. Target: reduce Meta dependency from 73% to below 65% within 12 months.
